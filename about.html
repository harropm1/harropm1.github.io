Enjoy The Ride

About Enjoy The Ride

Enjoy the Ride is a site for families, friends, or groups that will help them pick and rate their favorite Walt Disney World rides and attractions so they know what to prioritize on their trip. The goal is to help guests reduce stress while they are planning their trip and during their time at the park by knowing ahead of time what is most important and what can be skipped. By using Enjoy The Ride, various guests can rate the same ride multiple times, so if Mom and big brother love Mad Tea Party, and Dad and little sister think they might be sick when they go on it, all four of them will not have to wait for the ride while in Magic Kingdom.

About Walt Disney World

Magic Kingdom

Magic Kingdom opened on October 1, 1971, almost 5 years after Walt Disney died. It was built on top of a series of tunnels and storage areas, so it is technically on the second floor. This helps keep the six distinct lands in the park separate from each other, making their theming unique.

  • The first land, where guests enter, is Main Street, USA. On Main Street, USA, there is a train station, city hall, shops, and restaurants. At the end of Main Street, USA is the hub (a circle where the spokes go to the other lands) and Cinderella Castle.
  • The next land to the right is Adventureland, which represents the mystery of exploring foreign lands, inlcuding Africa, Asia, the Middle East, South America, the South Pacific, and the Caribbean.
  • Next is Frontierland, where guests experience the American Old West, including cowboys and the Rivers of America.
  • Liberty Square follows this and is themed after colonial America. The buildings follow an architectural progression, from the late 1600s to the late 1800s.
  • Fantasyland, the next land, is the largest section of the park. It includes many of the classic rides, but was recently expanded to include sections focused on Beauty and the Beast, Snow White, and The Little Mermaid.
  • Tomorrowland is the last section, and is set in an intergallactic city that was based on a 1950s concept of the future.

Epcot

Epcot opened on October 1, 1982. It has gone through many changes, especially in the section known as Future World. Originally Epcot was suppsed to be an experimental city, but Walt Disney was never able to get the funding for it. Guests typically enter in Future World, where they are greeted by the iconic Spaceship Earth. There pavillions to the left are based around science and innovation, while the pavillions to the right focus on the natural world. From here, guests enter the World Showcase, which houses 11 pavillions, focusing on the culture, architecture, landscapes, and cuisines of those countries. The current countries that are represented in the World Showcase are: Mexico, Norway, China, Germany, Italy, United States (known as The American Adventure), Japan, Morocco, France, United Kingdom, and Canada. Epcot also hosts many festivals throughout the year, focusing on food and drinks from countries all over the world, not just those in the World Showcase.

Hollywood Studios

Hollywood Studios opened on May 1, 1989. Hollywood Studios has also gone through many changes recently, especially with the addition of Toy Story Land, and the upcoming Star Wars land. This park is dedicated to the worlds of film, television, music, and theater. When Hollywood Studios opened, it was both a park and a production studio, with a functioning backlot and an animation studio. Many of these production locations, and the attractions around them, were removed in the late 2000s. Hollywood Studios also features 6 lands, including Hollywood Boulevard, Echo Lake, Grand Avenue, Toy Story Land, Animation Courtyard, and Sunset Boulevard. Along with rides, shops, and restaurants, Hollywood Studios offers more shows and live entertainment than other parks.

Animal Kingdom

Animal Kingdom opened on Earth Day, April 22, 1998. It is the largest park in the world, and is soley dedicated to the natural environment and animal conservation. Animal Kingdom has seven diferent areas.

  • Guests enter in Oasis, which is similar to Main Street, USA. This area features animals in zoo-like exhibits and vegetation and trees. This was done intentionally to separate guests from the outside world.
  • Discovery Island is the central hub, and features The Tree of Life, a 145-foot, man-made baobob tree that includes 325 carvings of existing and extinct animals.
  • Pandora - The World of Avatar is based on the movie series Avatar. The area features floating mountains, like in the movie, as well as alien wildlife and bioluminescent plants. The land took 6 years to design and build.
  • Africa, the next section, is set in the fictional village of Harambe. There are multiple wildlife areas in this section, including an 800 square mile area with a safari.
  • Rafiki's Planet Watch is only accessible by train from the Africa section, and includes a petting zoo, animal habitat conservation area, and animal care facilities.
  • Asia is set in the fictional kingdom of Anandapur. It includes details from Cambodia, India, Indonesia, Mongolia, Nepal, and Thailand. This area also includes nature walks, as well as Expeditiion Everest, a popular rollercoaster based around a Yeti.
  • Last is Dinoland U.S.A. This land is focused on dinosaurs and other prehistoric life. It includes a playground, a casting of the most complete dinosaur fossil ever found, and a carnival-style attraction area.

About Michelle

Michelle has been to Disney six times (1994, 2000, 2010, 2012, 2016, and 2018) with both family and friends. Her trips have included trying to get 10 people on a ride at the same time, and planning with two people who like rollercoasters and one who doesn't. This site came from a need to know which rides her friends and family preferred, so she could not only plan a basic route through each park, but also prevent backtracking.
